B1 Speaking Lesson
1. How to Express Your Opinion
At the B1 level, you need to express your opinions clearly and confidently. Use phrases like:
"In my opinion, ..."
"I think that ..."
"From my point of view, ..."
Tip: Start with a clear phrase to express your opinion. Then, give reasons for your opinion to make it stronger.
2. Agreeing and Disagreeing with Opinions
When someone shares their opinion, you may agree or disagree. Here are some useful expressions:
"I completely agree with you."
"That's a good point, but I think..."
"I disagree because..."
Tip: Use phrases like "I see your point," "I understand," or "That's true," when agreeing. Use "I don't think so" or "I'm not sure about that" for disagreement.
3. Giving Reasons for Your Opinion
To give more strength to your opinion, always try to explain why you think that way. You can use:
"Because..."
"For example..."
"This is because..."
Tip: Adding reasons makes your opinion clearer and helps the listener understand you better.
4. Asking for Others' Opinions
If you want to know what others think, you can ask:
"What do you think about...?"
"What's your opinion on...?"
"Do you agree with me?"
Tip: Use open-ended questions to encourage others to share their thoughts and opinions.
Step-by-Step: Starting a Discussion

Step 1: Start by stating your opinion. For example, "I think social media is a good way to stay connected."

Step 2: Provide a reason or example to explain your opinion. For example, "It allows people to communicate easily, even across long distances."

Step 3: Ask the other person for their opinion. For example, "What do you think about social media?"

Step 4: Agree or disagree with their opinion and continue the conversation.
