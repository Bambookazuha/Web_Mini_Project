A2 Speaking
1. Talking About Your Daily Routine
To talk about your daily activities, you can use the verb "do" or "have." Here are some examples:
"I wake up at 7 AM."
"I have breakfast at 8 AM."
"I go to work at 9 AM."
Tip: Remember to use the correct form of the verb. For example, "I wake up" or "I go to work." Use the present simple tense for daily routines.
2. Describing Your Hobbies
You can describe your hobbies by saying what you like doing. Use verbs like "enjoy," "love," or "hate."
"I enjoy playing soccer."
"I love reading books."
"I hate cleaning the house."
Tip: When you talk about hobbies, try to add more details. For example, "I love playing soccer with my friends" or "I enjoy reading mystery books."
3. Asking About Someone's Daily Routine
To ask someone about their daily routine, you can use questions like:
"What time do you wake up?"
"Do you go to work every day?"
"What do you usually do after work?"
Tip: Use "What time" for specific activities and "What do you usually do" for habits.
4. Talking About the Weekend
You can also talk about what you do on weekends. For example:
"On weekends, I like to relax at home."
"I go to the park on Saturdays."
"On Sundays, I meet my friends."
Tip: To talk about future plans, use "going to." For example, "I am going to visit my family this weekend."
Step-by-Step Example: Asking About Daily Routine

Step 1: Start by greeting the person: "Hello, how are you?"

Step 2: Ask, "What time do you wake up?"

Step 3: Listen to their answer and follow up with more questions like, "Do you go to work every day?"

Step 4: Share your own routine: "I wake up at 7 AM and go to work at 9 AM."
