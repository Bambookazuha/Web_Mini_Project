English Skills Exercises

A1 Level

Reading Comprehension

Passage: Sarah likes to play with her dog in the park. Every day, she takes her dog for a walk in the morning. Sarah loves the park because it is quiet and peaceful.

Questions:
  • What does Sarah like to do?
  • Where does she go every day?
  • Why does Sarah love the park?

Writing Exercise

Write about your best friend. Include:
  • What is their name?
  • What do they like to do?
  • Why do you like them?

Listening Comprehension

Listen to the audio (hypothetical scenario): "You hear someone talking about their favorite food. They say they like to eat pasta with tomato sauce."

Questions:
  • What is their favorite food?
  • What do they like to eat with the pasta?

Speaking Exercise

Talk about your daily routine. Include:
  • What time do you wake up?
  • What do you do in the morning?
  • What time do you go to bed?
A2 Level

Reading Comprehension

Passage: Mark is planning a weekend trip to the beach with his family. He will leave on Saturday morning and return on Sunday evening. He is very excited because he loves swimming and playing volleyball with his friends.

Questions:
  • When is Mark leaving for the beach?
  • What does he enjoy doing at the beach?

Writing Exercise

Write about your last holiday. Include:
  • Where did you go?
  • What did you do there?
  • What was the best part of your holiday?

Listening Comprehension

Listen to the audio (hypothetical scenario): "You hear someone talking about their weekend activities. They mention going to the gym, meeting friends for lunch, and watching a movie in the evening."

Questions:
  • What did the person do on the weekend?
  • What did they do in the evening?

Speaking Exercise

Describe your hometown. Include:
  • What is it like?
  • What are the most interesting places?
  • What do you like to do there?
B1 Level

Reading Comprehension

Passage: Rachel enjoys reading books about history. She has read many books on ancient civilizations. One of her favorites is a book about Ancient Egypt. She finds it interesting to learn about how the Egyptians built the pyramids and how they lived thousands of years ago.

Questions:
  • What kind of books does Rachel enjoy?
  • What is one of her favorite books about?

Writing Exercise

Write about your plans for the future. Include:
  • What do you want to do in the next 5 years?
  • What are your career goals?

Listening Comprehension

Listen to the audio (hypothetical scenario): "You hear a conversation between two people. One person is asking about the other person’s plans for the weekend. They mention they are going to visit a museum and then have dinner with friends."

Questions:
  • What are the person’s plans for the weekend?
  • What will they do after visiting the museum?

Speaking Exercise

Talk about your favorite movie. Include:
  • What is the movie about?
  • Why do you like it?
  • Who are the main characters?
B2 Level

Reading Comprehension

Passage: John has always dreamed of traveling the world. He has visited many countries in Europe, including Italy and Spain. This year, he plans to visit Japan and China. He is particularly excited about experiencing different cultures and trying new foods.

Questions:
  • Where has John traveled?
  • What countries does he plan to visit this year?
  • What is John excited about?

Writing Exercise

Write a letter to a friend inviting them to your birthday party. Include:
  • The date and time of the party
  • What activities will be there
  • What food and drinks will be available

Listening Comprehension

Listen to the audio (hypothetical scenario): "You hear a conversation between two people discussing their upcoming trip. They talk about packing, the flights, and the excitement of visiting a new city."

Questions:
  • What are the people talking about?
  • What aspects of the trip do they discuss?

Speaking Exercise

Discuss an important decision you made recently. Include:
  • What decision did you make?
  • Why was it important?
  • How did you feel afterward?
C1 Level

Reading Comprehension

Passage: The influence of social media on teenagers has been a topic of debate for many years. While some argue that it helps young people stay connected and informed, others believe it has detrimental effects on their mental health. Recent studies have shown a strong correlation between excessive social media use and feelings of isolation and anxiety.

Questions:
  • What are the two views on social media’s influence on teenagers?
  • What have recent studies shown about social media use?

Writing Exercise

Write an essay discussing the impact of social media on society. Include:
  • The positive aspects of social media
  • The negative aspects of social media
  • Your opinion on the overall impact of social media

Listening Comprehension

Listen to the audio (hypothetical scenario): "You hear a podcast discussing the challenges and opportunities presented by artificial intelligence. The hosts talk about its potential benefits and risks."

Questions:
  • What is the podcast about?
  • What do the hosts discuss?

Speaking Exercise

Present your opinion on the impact of artificial intelligence. Include:
  • What are the main benefits?
  • What are the main risks?
  • Do you think AI will be more beneficial or harmful in the future?
C2 Level

Reading Comprehension

Passage: The rapid advancement of technology has brought about significant changes in the global workforce. While some industries have been transformed by automation and artificial intelligence, others have emerged, creating new opportunities. However, this technological revolution has raised concerns about job displacement and the need for upskilling in various sectors.

Questions:
  • How has technology changed the global workforce?
  • What concerns have been raised about this change?

Writing Exercise

Write a critical analysis of the role of technology in modern education. Include:
  • How technology has changed the way we learn
  • The advantages and disadvantages of using technology in education
  • Your opinion on the future of technology in education

Listening Comprehension

Listen to the audio (hypothetical scenario): "You hear a lecture on the ethical implications of genetic engineering. The speaker discusses both the potential benefits and the moral concerns surrounding gene editing technology."

Questions:
  • What is the lecture about?
  • What are the two sides of the ethical debate mentioned?

Speaking Exercise

Debate the ethics of genetic engineering. Include:
  • What are the potential benefits?
  • What are the ethical concerns?
  • Do you think genetic engineering should be regulated? If so, how?
