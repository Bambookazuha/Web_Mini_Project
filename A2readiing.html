A2 Reading

Improve your reading with us!

Lesson 1: A Trip to the Museum

Learn common vocabulary and phrases when going on a trip.

Text:
Last weekend, I visited the museum with my friends. The museum had many interesting exhibits, such as old paintings and sculptures. We spent most of our time in the art section. My favorite part was the ancient Egyptian display, where I saw mummies and old artifacts. After the museum tour, we had lunch at a nearby cafe. It was a fun day, and I learned a lot.

Comprehension Questions:
1. Where did the author go last weekend?
2. What did the museum have?
3. Which part of the museum was the author’s favorite?

Lesson 2: The Farm

Learn words and phrases used at the farm.

Text:
On the farm, there are many animals. There are cows that give us milk, chickens that lay eggs, and sheep that produce wool. The farmer takes care of the animals every day. In the morning, the cows are milked, and the eggs are collected. The sheep are sheared in the spring for their wool. I enjoy visiting the farm because it’s fun to see all the animals.

Comprehension Questions:
1. What animals are found on the farm?
2. What do the cows give us?
3. What happens to the sheep in the spring?

Lesson 3: At the Park

Practice understanding the passage talking about a visit to the park.

Text:
Today, I went to the park with my friends. We played soccer, rode bikes, and had a picnic. I like to visit the park because there is so much to do. There is a playground for little children, and there are benches where adults can sit and relax. The weather was perfect, so we stayed for a few hours before going home.

Comprehension Questions:
1. Where did the author go today?
2. What can little children do at the park?
3. What can adults do at the park?

Lesson 4: Healthy Eating

Learn to read the passage about healthy eating..

Text:
Eating healthy food is important for staying strong and energetic. Fruits, vegetables, and whole grains should be part of every meal. Eating a variety of foods helps our bodies get all the nutrients we need. It’s also important to drink plenty of water and avoid sugary drinks. Regular exercise, along with healthy eating, is the best way to keep your body in good shape

Comprehension Questions:
1. What types of food should be part of every meal?
2. What is the best way to keep your body in good shape?
3. What should we drink instead of sugary drinks?

Lesson 5:The Library

Learn to understand the description of a library.

Text:
The library is a quiet place where people can borrow books. There are many types of books, including novels, magazines, and reference books. I like to visit the library after school to find new books to read. The library also has computers for research, and sometimes they hold events like story hours for children. It's a great place to learn and relax.

Comprehension Questions:
1. What types of books are available in the library?
2. What kind of events does the library hold?

Lesson 6:The Weather

Learn how to describe the weather.

Text:
The weather can change every day. Some days are sunny, while others are cloudy or rainy. In the winter, it can be very cold, and sometimes it snows. In the summer, the days are warm, and we often go to the beach. It’s important to check the weather forecast before making plans, so we know what to wear and what activities to do.

Comprehension Questions:
1. What can happen to the weather every day?
2. Why is it important to check the weather forecast?
3. What should we know before making plans?
4. What is the weather like in winter?

Lesson 7: The Weekend

Practice reading the passage talking about the author's opinion on weekend.

Text:
I love the weekend because I can relax and do things I enjoy. On Saturdays, I like to go shopping with my friends, and we usually have lunch at a café. Sundays are my rest days, so I spend time with my family. We often watch movies, cook together, and talk. The weekend is a special time for me.

Comprehension Questions:
1. Why does the author love the weekend?
2. What does the author do on Sundays?
3. What activities does the author do with their family?

Lesson 8: My Best Friend

Learn how to describe a person.

Text:
My best friend is Mia. She is kind and always helps me with my homework. We like to play games and read books together. Mia has long brown hair and a big smile. Every Saturday, we ride our bikes to the park. She loves ice cream, and her favorite flavor is chocolate. I am lucky to have a friend like her.

Comprehension Questions:
1. What is the name of the writer's best friend?
2. What is the writer's best friend's favorite ice cream flavor?
3.What do they do together?

Lesson 9: The Coffee Shop

Try understanding the description of a visit to a coffee shop.

Text:
I visit the coffee shop every Friday. I like to drink hot chocolate and eat a muffin. The shop is quiet, and I sit by the window. Sometimes, I read a book or talk with friends. It is a nice place to relax.

Comprehension Questions:
1. When does the writer visit the coffee shop?
2. What does the writer drink?
3.Where does the writer sit?
