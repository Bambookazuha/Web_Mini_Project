How to Start a Conversation
Starting a conversation in English can be easy! Use these simple steps to begin:
  1. Greeting: Start with a simple greeting like "Hello" or "Hi".
  2. Introduce yourself: Say "My name is [your name]." or "Iâ€™m [your name]."
  3. Ask questions: After you introduce yourself, ask the other person their name: "What is your name?"
  4. Ask about where they are from: You can also ask: "Where are you from?"
  5. Show interest: You can show interest by asking questions about their day, or where they live, etc.
1. Introducing Yourself
When you meet someone, you can introduce yourself using a simple sentence:
"Hello, my name is ______."
Tip: Keep your introduction simple. You can say, "Hi, my name is [your name]." Try to speak clearly and slowly.
2. Asking for Someone's Name
To ask someone's name, you can say:
"What is your name?"
Tip: You can also say, "What's your name?" It is the same as "What is your name?" but sounds a little shorter and more informal.
3. Simple Questions and Answers
After introducing yourself, you can ask basic questions to learn more about someone:
"How are you?" or "Where are you from?"

Step 1: Start by asking, "How are you?"

Step 2: Wait for their response, such as "I'm good, thank you!"

Step 3: Continue the conversation by asking, "Where are you from?"

4. Talking About Where You Are From
You can tell someone where you are from by saying:
"I am from ______."
Tip: Practice saying your country or city. For example, "I am from New York" or "I am from Thailand."
5. Talking About Your Job or Studies
If someone asks about your job or studies, you can say:
"I am a student." or "I work as a teacher."
Tip: If you are a student, you can say, "I am a student at [name of your school]."
