A1 Writing

Improve your writing with us!

How to structure a Simple Sentence

A simple sentence typically consists of two parts. The two parts are subject and predicate. The subject tells us who or what the sentence is about, and the predicate tells us what the subject does or is.

Example 1:
My dog barks.
- **Subject:** My dog
- **Predicate:** barks
Example 2:
The sun shines brightly.
- **Subject:** The sun
- **Predicate:** shines brightly

1. Introduction to Simple Sentences

Learn how to form simple sentences using common subjects.

  • Example: "I am a student."
  • Example: "This is a book."

2. Self-Introduction Practice

Introduce yourself by writing about your name, age, and interests.

  • Example: "My name is Sara. I am 10 years old. I like reading books."
  • Example: "My name is Dino. I am 14 years old. I like playing games."

3. Describing Objects and People

Practice describing common objects or people around you.

  • Example: "This is a red apple."
  • Example: "She has brown hair and green eyes."

4. Writing About Daily Activities

Write simple sentences about your daily activities and routines.

  • Example: "I wake up at 7 am."
  • Example: "I go to school at 8 am."

5. Basic Descriptions of Places

Describe places you know, like your home, school, or favorite park.

  • Example: "My school is big and has many classrooms."
  • Example: "The park is green with lots of trees."

6. Talking about hobbies

Describe a hobby or activity you enjoy in your free time.

  • Example: "I like swimming. It is fun and helps me stay healthy."
  • Example: "My favorite hobby is drawing. I draw pictures of animals."

How to form simple questions

A question typically begins with a question word or a helping verb. It often includes a subject and a main verb. Question words like what, where, when, why, how, and who help provide specific information.

Example 1:
What is your name?
- **Question Word:** What
- **Helping Verb:** is
- **Subject:** your name
Example 2:
Where do you live?
- ** Question Word:** Where
- **Helping Verb:** do
- **Subject:** you
- **Main Verb:** live

1. WH-Question Formation Practice

Learn how to form questions using WH-words.

  • Example:
    • Statement:"He is eating lunch." Question: "What is he eating?"
Statement 1:"She is talking to her friend." Statement 2: "They are watching a movie."

2. Yes/No Question Practice

Practice forming Yes/No questions by using helping verbs.

  • Example:
    • Statement:"She likes chocolate." Question: "Does she like chocolate?"
Statement 1:"He is playing football." Statement 2: "You went to the library."

3. Rearranging the words

Rearrange the words in correct form.

  • Example:
    • Given Statement:"you/are/happy?" Arranged: "Are you happy?"

1. is/she/coming/when?

2. like/they/do/ice cream?

4. Tag question practice

Add question tags to the given sentences.

  • Example:
    • Given Statement:"She is kind." Question: "She is kind,isn't she?"

1. You are a student.

2. They will come to the party.

5. Identify the Question Word

Choose the correct question word.

  • Example:
    • Question:"___ is your favourite color?" Correct Answer: "What"

1. ___ are you going tomorrow?

2. ___ is knocking on the door?

6. Form Questions to Answers

Form questions to the given answers.

  • Example:
    • Answer:"I like apple." Question: "What do you like?"

1. I am 12 years old.

2. She lives in paris.
