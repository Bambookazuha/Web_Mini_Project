B2 Speaking
1. Introduction: Debate Skills
In B2-level speaking, you should be able to participate in debates and discussions. At this level, it's important to express your opinions and support them with well-structured arguments. Being able to discuss topics clearly and respectfully is a key skill.
2. Giving Strong Arguments
To give a strong argument, you need to state your opinion clearly and explain why you believe it. Use these phrases to express your arguments:
"I believe that..."
"In my opinion..."
"I think it's important to..."
3. Providing Reasons and Examples
It's important to provide reasons and examples when giving an argument. This makes your opinion more convincing and shows that you have thought carefully about the topic. Here are some ways to introduce your reasons:
  • "The reason I think this is..."
  • "For example..."
  • "Let me give you an example..."
4. Refuting Counter-Arguments
Sometimes you need to refute or challenge a counter-argument. You can do this politely by using phrases like:
"I understand your point, but..."
"That's true, but I would argue that..."
"You make a good point, however..."
Tips for Debating
  • Listen carefully to the other person's opinion.
  • Stay calm and avoid emotional reactions.
  • Use examples to make your points clearer.
  • Respect the opinions of others, even if you disagree.
5. Practice: Discuss a Topic
Now it's time to practice! Choose a topic from the list below and discuss it with a partner. Remember to state your opinion clearly, provide reasons and examples, and refute counter-arguments politely.
  • Should students wear school uniforms?
  • Is social media harmful to young people?
  • Should the government increase taxes on unhealthy food?
