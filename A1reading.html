A1 Reading

Improve your reading with us!

  • Start with simple texts that use common vocabulary.
  • Pay attention to pictures and titles.
  • Focus on key words rather than understanding every word.
  • Read aloud to improve pronunciation and fluency.
  • Practice reading every day to build confidence and speed.
  • Ask questions after reading.

Lesson 1: Daily Routines

Learn common vocabulary and phrases about daily activities. Read about Tom's typical day.

Text:
Tom wakes up at 7:00 AM. He has breakfast at 7:30 AM. He goes to work at 8:00 AM. He finishes work at 5:00 PM. In the evening, he relaxes and watches TV. At 10:00 PM, he goes to bed.

Comprehension Questions:
1. What time does Tom wake up?
2. What does Tom do in the evening?
3. When does Tom go to bed?

Lesson 2: At the Supermarket

Learn words and phrases used for shopping. Read a short dialogue between a shopper and a cashier.

Text:
Shopper: Excuse me, how much is this apple?
Cashier: It's $1.50.
Shopper: I'll take two, please.
Cashier: That will be $3.00. Would you like a bag?
Shopper: Yes, please.

Comprehension Questions:
1. How much does the apple cost?
2. How many apples does the shopper buy?
3. Does the shopper want a bag?

Lesson 3: Weather Report

Practice understanding simple weather reports. Read a short forecast for tomorrow.

Text:
Tomorrow's weather will be sunny with a temperature of 25°C. There will be no rain, and the wind will be light. The evening will be cool, with temperatures dropping to 15°C.

Comprehension Questions:
1. What is the temperature tomorrow?
2. Will there be rain?
3. What will the temperature be in the evening?

Lesson 4: Traveling by Train

Learn to read train schedules and tickets. Improve your reading with real-world examples.

Text:
The train from London to Manchester leaves at 9:30 AM. It will stop at Birmingham at 10:45 AM and arrive in Manchester at 12:00 PM. The cost of a one-way ticket is £15.50.

Comprehension Questions:
1. What time does the train leave London?
2. What time will the train arrive in Manchester?
3. How much does a ticket cost?

Lesson 5: Visiting the Zoo

Learn to understand the description of a past visit.

Text:
Yesterday, I went to the zoo with my family. We saw lions, elephants, and monkeys. My favorite animal was the giraffe because it was so tall. We had a great time!

Comprehension Questions:
1. Where did they go?
2. What was the writer's favorite animal?

Lesson 6: My Pet Cat

Learn how to describe your pet by talking about its appearance and habits.

Text:
I have a pet cat named Bella. She is small and white with blue eyes. Bella loves to play with her toys and run around the house. Every morning, I feed her milk and fish. She sleeps on a soft bed near my window. Sometimes, Bella sits with me when I do my homework. She is my best friend, and I love her very much.

Comprehension Questions:
1. What is the cat's name?
2. What does Bella eat?
3.Where does Bella sleep?
Who is Bella's best friend?

Lesson 7: A Day At The Beach

Practice reading the description of a fun trip.

Text:
Last weekend, I went to the beach with my family. The weather was sunny and warm. We played volleyball and built sandcastles. My brother swam in the sea, but I just walked along the shore. For lunch, we had sandwiches and fruit. In the evening, we watched the sunset. It was a fun and relaxing day.

Comprehension Questions:
1. What did they do at the beach?
2. What did they eat for lunch?
3.How was the weather?

Lesson 8: My Best Friend

Learn how to describe a person.

Text:
My best friend is Mia. She is kind and always helps me with my homework. We like to play games and read books together. Mia has long brown hair and a big smile. Every Saturday, we ride our bikes to the park. She loves ice cream, and her favorite flavor is chocolate. I am lucky to have a friend like her.

Comprehension Questions:
1. What is the name of the writer's best friend?
2. What is the writer's best friend's favorite ice cream flavor?
3.What do they do together?

Lesson 9: The Coffee Shop

Try understanding the description of a visit to a coffee shop.

Text:
I visit the coffee shop every Friday. I like to drink hot chocolate and eat a muffin. The shop is quiet, and I sit by the window. Sometimes, I read a book or talk with friends. It is a nice place to relax.

Comprehension Questions:
1. When does the writer visit the coffee shop?
2. What does the writer drink?
3.Where does the writer sit?
