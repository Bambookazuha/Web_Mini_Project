C1 Advanced Speaking
1. Introduction to Persuasion
At the C1 level, speaking skills involve making well-supported arguments, using advanced techniques to persuade and influence others. You should be able to express complex ideas clearly, supporting your claims with facts, logic, and emotional appeal. This lesson will guide you through the art of argumentation and persuasion.
2. Structuring a Persuasive Argument
To make a persuasive argument, you need to structure your ideas logically. A good argument should include:
- **Clear Statement of Opinion**: Start by clearly stating your opinion.
- **Evidence**: Use evidence or examples to support your claim.
- **Counter-Argument**: Acknowledge the opposite view, and explain why it is weaker.
- **Conclusion**: End with a strong conclusion that reinforces your position.
3. Persuasion Techniques
When persuading someone, it's essential to appeal to both **logic** and **emotion**. Here are some effective persuasion techniques:
  • Pathos (Emotional Appeal): Evoke feelings to connect with your audience on a personal level.
  • Logos (Logical Appeal): Use facts, statistics, and rational arguments to support your position.
  • Ethos (Credibility Appeal): Show that you are trustworthy and knowledgeable.
4. Example of a Persuasive Argument
Here's an example of a persuasive argument on the topic of **climate change**:
"I believe that climate change is one of the most pressing issues of our time. The evidence is clear: scientists worldwide agree that human activities, such as burning fossil fuels, are contributing to global warming. For example, recent studies show a dramatic rise in global temperatures over the past century. While some argue that climate change is part of a natural cycle, the speed and scale of the changes we are seeing today are unprecedented. The consequences of inaction could be catastrophic, affecting agriculture, sea levels, and human health. Therefore, it's crucial that we take immediate action to reduce emissions and shift towards sustainable energy sources."
